Cost: $50 per month/10$ for first class

Taking your first class?

Students new to belly dance begin with a class called the "Fundamentals of Bellydance". During this twelve week course, dancers learn the moves basic to belly dance, how to combine those moves and ways to travel across the dance floor.

There are two classes to choose from: Tuesdays from 5:30pm to 6:30pm, or Wednesdays from 6:15pm to 7:15pm. The same content is taught in each class, so you can alternate between classes and remain on schedule. If you do miss a class, it's generally not a problem as there is a lot of review built into every class.

Sign up for twelve weeks of classes after taking your first class and receive a 20$ discount!

The fee for the twelve weeks is $100 for students that pay the first day of class. That is a $20 discount from the standard price is $120. If you begin during weeks two through six, and pay for the remainder of the course, the fee is $9 per class, and after that, classes are $10 each.

Class Schedule

Mondays

Intermediate Class

6:15 - 7:15 pm

Fan Veil Routine
Music: Zi Zi's Journey, Artist: Lindsey Stirling
Fan Veils are a gorgeous prop and look like they have a life of their own under the stage lights! While we will incorporate belly dance moves in this routine, the focus will be on the prop. Fan veil experience is not a prerequisite. Students in this class have had at least four years of previous dance experience.

Advanced Class

7:30 - 8:30 pm

Routine: TBA

Music: Like a River, Artist: Bishop Braggs

Formally troupe class, this class is now open to anyone interested in an advanced level Hourglass class. Students move forward at different paces, but generally, this class is most suitable for students who have had at least five or six years of previous belly dance experience. Additionally, it is advised (but not required) that students participating in this class also take the Int/Advanced Drills & Zills Class on Tuesdays - 7:30 to 8:30 pm.

Fundamentals of Bellydance

5:30 - 6:30 pm

For students new to belly dance. This twelve week course introduces dancers to the moves basic to belly dance. Students will learn how to combine moves and travel across the dance floor.

Intermediate/Advanced Drills

6:45 - 7:45 pm

During drills classes, students will practice level appropriate belly dance moves repeatedly so as to develop muscle strength, muscle memory and coordination skills. Dancers will also practice traveling and finger cymbal skills.

Finger Cymbal Routine, Music: An Eznak Ya M'allem, Artist: Rola

Intermediate Class

Classic Routine, Music: Mally Wanna Mally, Artist: Leila
The song Mally Wanna Mally or Tab Wana Mali was first made popular in 2001 by the legendary singer Warda. Along with Lebanon's Fayrouz and Egypt's late Oum Kalthoum, Warda was one of the legendary singers of the Arab world, with a voice that has been described as both sweet and powerful. The majority of students in this class have had four to ten years of previous belly dance experience.
